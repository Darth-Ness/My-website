Objections drawn from language learning apps

Duolingo, Babbel, Rosetta Stone, all of these are mobile or web applications
for simplifying the process of learning a foreign language. However, many such
applications simply the process to much. In this post, I will outline my objections
to these applications, then explain my process to follow instead.
(Do note, both my objections and process are orientated for Latin alphabet based languages.
and may not necessarily work reflect languages that use other systems.)

The first objection is the fact that applications often teach nouns way more often than other
types of words such as verbs or adjectives. This will give an improper grasp of a language.
Second, most of these applications use the "Parrot learning method", or these applications teach
you a language like you would teach a parrot. This will lead to poor understanding of phonics,
grammar, or more unique quirks of a language. Finally, these applications often teach a very
formal form of a language.

Here's what I do instead.
1.Get a grasp on the alphabet,
2.Practice your language's phonics.
3.Learn pronouns.
4.Practice common verbs and their different tenses.
5.Learn about other unique, grammatical quirks of a language.
